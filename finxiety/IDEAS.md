# Finxiety / Finxiety — Original Ideas

_Brainstorm document. Source material for [[Finxiety — Product Backlog]]. Organized as a portfolio of invention families under Tsunam.ai._

---

I like treating this as a portfolio rather than a startup. That's a very inventor mindset.

Instead of asking, "What's my company?" ask, "What collection of inventions belong under Tsunam.ai?"

If I look at the themes I've repeatedly returned to over the last few months — financial decision-making, inequality, reducing confusion, helping people navigate complex systems — I see a few natural product families.

## PM Prioritization Framework

I wouldn't prioritize based on social impact alone.

I wouldn't prioritize based on revenue alone.

I'd prioritize based on three factors:

| Idea | User Pain | Data Availability | Build Effort |
|------|-----------|------------------|--------------|
| Rent vs. Buy | High | High | Medium |
| Home Affordability | High | High | Low |
| Benefit Cliff Detector | Very High | Medium | High |
| Emergency Fund Calculator | High | High | Low |
| Healthy Meals by Budget | High | Medium | Medium |
| Cash Flow Survival Planner | Very High | High | Medium |

The sweet spot: high pain + high data availability + low build effort.

**First five invention candidates:**

1. Home Affordability Reality Calculator
2. Emergency Fund Calculator
3. Cash Flow Survival Planner
4. Rent vs. Buy Simulator
5. Healthy Meals by Budget

Why? Because all five solve real problems, can be built by one person, and can be released as standalone tools.

## The Underlying Mission

What I find most interesting is that this portfolio is actually very coherent. At first glance it looks like dozens of unrelated ideas.

But the underlying mission is remarkably consistent:

> Help ordinary people make better decisions when the world is too complicated.

That's a much tighter invention thesis than "build AI products."

Under that framing, Tsunam.ai starts looking less like a startup and more like a public-interest invention lab. Each tool is a small experiment in reducing confusion and increasing agency. Over time, one or two of those inventions may emerge as much larger opportunities. But you don't need to know which ones in advance. That's the advantage of a portfolio approach.

## Community Infrastructure Tools (→ Finxiety)

Now we're getting into a different category.

The first set of ideas were consumer tools.

This set is closer to what I'd call community infrastructure tools.

These aren't necessarily aimed at the individual navigating a difficult financial situation. They're aimed at helping people navigate the ecosystem around them — or helping the organizations that serve them.

That's actually a very interesting niche because many nonprofits and community organizations are underfunded and using surprisingly primitive tools.

---

> You may qualify for:
> - SNAP
> - Utility assistance

That's not revolutionary technology. But it could have a very real effect on someone's life.

## The Core Insight

The most valuable inventions in this space are often not calculators at all. They're clarity engines.

The user isn't saying: *calculate something.*

They're saying: *I don't understand my options.*

The invention is a way of turning a complicated system into a simple next step. And that theme — reducing confusion in complex systems — shows up over and over again in the kinds of problems that matter most.
